1973

In 1973 brother and sister “DJ Kool Herc” and Campbell Clyde threw a party inside their apartment’s rec room so she could raise money to buy her kids new clothes. DJ Kool Herc looped the drum breaks of disco songs by using two record players and rewinding one while the other one played. The party was a massive success with over 300 people showing up So DJ Kool Herc threw more parties, eventually enlisting the help of Coke La Rock for announcements, adlibs and eventually some of the first rapping.

1979

By 1979 Rapping was gaining popularity through the Bronx party scene, but the first rap wasn’t recorded until the record executive Sylvia Robinson gathered the best rappers of the time into a group called “The Sugarhill Gang” to record “Rapper’s Delight”.

1980

In 1980 Roland released TR808, a failed product that became a hip hop essential after hiphop OG Afrika Bambataa and Soulsonic Force used it in their song “Planet Rock”.

1982

In 1982 Sylvia Johnson (the record executive behind The Sugarhill Gang) had the idea of writing a song about the struggles of living in the Bronx. She got a local poet to write it. It was performed by Grandmaster Flash and The Furious Five. This song set the tone for most rap going forward.

1990

In 1990 Vanilla Ice released the song “Ice Ice Baby” which was the first hip hop song to reach the top of the billboard hot 100. This was the song that introduced hip hop to the public. Suge Knight of Death Row Records saw the success this song was getting and wanted a piece of it. Suge Knight and his entourage saw Vanilla Ice in a restaurant in LA. “It was very intimidating to see these guys who were bigger than my bodyguards you know and a bunch of them. They pretty much grabbed my bodyguard and pulled him out and sat down there right next to me.” Suge Knight threatened to push Vanilla Ice off of a balcony if he didn't give him royalties.

1996

In 1996 Lil Kim released the album “Hard Core”. This album was what created the style of sexually charged lyrics that lead to Missy Elliot and Nicki Minaj.

2001

In 2001 Jay Z signed Kanye as a producer and writer. Kanye’s catchy melody-focused production with Jay Z’s complex rhymes created one of the most successful hip hop albums of the time. It debuted at number one and stayed there for three weeks.

2008

After a public breakup and the death of his mother, Kanye turns to music to deal with the pain. He takes the melodic and vulnerable rap he’s known for and takes it even further by singing the choruses. Because of the minimalist sound and the tr808 he finished the album “808s and Heartbreaks” in less than a month.

2010

In 2010 Drake released his debut album titled “Thank Me Later” which built upon the foundation set by “808s and Heartbreaks”. It was a fusion of hip hop and pop featuring introspective lyrics.

2012

At age 16 Chief Keef created “Don’t Like”, the song that introduced the drill genre to the public. The sound is characterized by aggressive lyrics and hard hitting 808-style bass. In the song Chief Keef says “A pop bitch, that’s that shit I don’t like” criticizing the fusion of pop and hip hop that was popular at the time.

2019

At 19 years old Lil Nas X got kicked out of the house and had to fend for himself. All he had was a meme page and a copy of Logic pro. He downloaded a beat from YouTube and recorded a song about “a lonely cowboy trying to run away from it all”. He promoted it through memes on Twitter and a new app called TikTok. He asked country singer Billy Ray Cyrus to be on the remix so he could introduce his music to a wider audience. Billy Ray Cyrus agreed and the song got a billion streams in less than a year.

2023

Lil Durk released “Almost Healed”, an album combining soul, hip hop, and country which might be the biggest album of his career.
